Constraints in model

              
    
            
    
          
    

          
    
            
    Mathematical constraints
    
            
    
              
    
                Plugboard is an involution: applying it twice is
                the identity. Each wire swaps two letters.
              
    
              
    
                Reflector pairs letters without fixed points: it
                is an involution with no letter mapping to itself.
              
    
              
    
                Each rotor applies a fixed permutation of the
                alphabet, observed through a window that advances with stepping and ring offsets.
              
    
              
    
                Enigma never maps a letter to itself — a crib
                gives equations linking plaintext and ciphertext symbols under unknown rotor state.
              
    
              
    
                Z3 searches for a satisfying assignment to
                integer/bit constraints that encodes wiring, stepping, and the crib consistency.
